# EPISTEMOLOGICAL AUDIT: The 1901 MacDougall Soul Color Measurement

## 1. Question Validity
- **Status:** Pseudo-Question / Category Error
- **Analysis:** The inquiry contains a fundamental historical and logical category error. The 1901 experiment conducted by Dr. Duncan MacDougall (published in 1907 in *American Medicine*) was strictly a volumetric and gravimetric study focused on the **mass** (weight) of the soul. It did not utilize any optical or spectral equipment to measure "color." The request seeks an "exact" value for a variable that was not part of the experimental design.

## 2. Reasoning Trace Verification
- **Trace Breakdown:**
  1. **Premise:** MacDougall measured the soul in 1901. (Historically True)
  2. **Premise:** Measurements include properties like weight and color. (Theoretically True, but not in this instance)
  3. **Conclusion:** There is an "exact color" from the 1901 study. (False)
- **Consistency Check:** The logic is internally inconsistent with the historical record. You cannot derive a visual property (color) from a purely gravimetric methodology (weighing patients on industrial scales).
- **Formal Proof Status:** The claim is non-verifiable. A review of the 1907 publication *The Soul: Hypothesis Concerning Soul Substance Together with Experimental Evidence of the Existence of Such Substance* confirms no mention of color measurements.

## 3. Hallucination Risk Assessment
- **Statistical Extrapolations:** The inquiry likely conflates the 1901 experiment with MacDougall’s later **1911** experiments, where he attempted to photograph the soul using x-ray and light-sensitive plates. In those later accounts, he described the soul as a **"light resembling that of the interstellar ether."**
- **Information Gaps:** There is no "exact color" recorded in 1901. Cultural associations of "blue" or "violet" are derived from the 1911 photography attempts or the contemporary work of Dr. Walter Kilner (1911) on the "human aura" using dicyanin dyes.
- **Human Metric Check:** A human researcher can verify the absence of color data in the 1901 primary source documents. Any assertion of an "exact color" from the 1901 study is a **historical hallucination.**

## 4. Final Verdict
- **Logic Score:** 1/10 (The question is built on a false premise).
- **Empirical Grounding:** **Hallucinatory** (Regarding the 1901 date) / **Speculative** (Regarding the 1911 description).
- **Recommendation:** Reframe the inquiry to distinguish between the **1901 Weight Study** (21 grams) and the **1911 Visual Hypothesis** (interstellar ether/blue light).
